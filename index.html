    EXPERIENCE

    FRC - First Robotics Competition

    FRC has taught me a variety of hands on skills such as using drill presses, band saws, milling machines and much, much more. FRC has also let me apply the skills I learned in the classroom to physical products.

    My role in the 1246 FRC team was overall/design lead and thus I was responsible for inetgrating CAD models into the final design, providing task for members of the team and designing the elevator, powercell (balls) storage system and th hooking mechanism for the climb.

    Probably one of my proudest achievements was using Fusion 360 to design a mechanical single use hook for Team 1246 FRC robot. A single use hook was needed to ensure the elevator would not descend during power off period at end of game. The mechanism utilizes spare parts found workshop and a custom milled aluminum hook. A spring was used such that no electrical components were required to latch on to the elevator.

    PROJECTS

    Computer Mouse Body

    Used Solidworks form tools to design an original sleek and ergonomic computer mouse body to be 3D printed. Constraints included designing around a circuit board from a seperate mouse and designing for 3D printability. The final result is the mouse I use to this day! Check out the CAD files here!

    Conveyor Assembly

    Used Solidworks and imported CAD models from McMaster-Carr to create a large modular conveyor assembly with sorting abilities. The conveyor was designed with the specifications of needing to transport 20x20x20cm and 10x10x30cm boxes with a max weight of 8.48kg. Check out the CAD files here!

    Goose Escape Game

    Used C++ and BearlibTerminal to program an turnbased endless roguelike dungeon game with randomly generated walls and enemies. The deeper you go the more enemies and walls you spawn you'll collect bird feed along the way which allow you to pause all enemy movement. How deep can you go before you are overwelmed by enemies?
